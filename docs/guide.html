1. Getting Started

Tic Tac Tone turns the classic tic-tac-toe game into a music-making experience. Every move on the 3×3 grid activates a synthesized instrument — kick, snare, bass, keys, pads, and more.

Two players take turns claiming pads. Each claimed square adds a new layer to a looping beat that builds as the game progresses. Get three in a row to win — and hear your winning instruments featured in a special break.

First tap: Audio starts when you make your first move. Make sure your device is not on silent mode.

2. Game Modes

Tap the MODE button to cycle through four modes:

2P — Two Player

Two players share the device, taking turns as X and O. Each move claims a pad and activates its instrument. Three in a row wins.

AI — vs Computer

Play as X against the computer (O). The AI uses a smart strategy to challenge you. The AI takes its turn automatically after a short delay.

JAM — Free Sampler

No rules, no turns, no winning. Tap any pad to toggle it on or off. Build up layers freely and experiment with different combinations. Great for creative exploration.

EDIT — Pattern Editor

Select any pad to edit its 16-step sequencer pattern. Change which steps are active, adjust note pitches for melodic instruments, and craft your own beats. See Edit Mode for full details.

4. Winning & Break

Get three of your marks in a row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — to win. An animated line draws across your winning cells.

The Break

When someone wins, the sequencer enters a 32-step break. During the break, only the three winning instruments play — a stripped-back moment that highlights your winning combination before the game resets.

Scoring

Scores track across multiple games: Player X wins, Player O wins, and Draws. Use the RESET button to clear all scores and start fresh.

Draw

If all 9 cells are claimed with no winner, it's a draw. All instruments keep playing — a full 9-layer mix.

6. Controls

The control row sits below the game buttons. Drag vertically on knobs to adjust their values.

BPM (40–200)

Controls the tempo of the sequencer. Drag up to increase, down to decrease. The current BPM displays in the header.

TAP Tempo

Tap the TAP button 3 or more times in rhythm to set the BPM. Useful for matching external music or setting a feel by hand.

Swing (0–100%)

Adds shuffle to the rhythm by delaying every other step. At 0% the beat is straight; higher values create a more laid-back, swung feel.

Filter

Sweep the frequency filter across all instruments. Turn left for a lowpass sweep (muffled), center for off, right for highpass (thin/bright).

Feel

Toggles humanized micro-timing. When on, each instrument gets subtle timing variations that make the beat feel more natural and less robotic.

Mode

Cycles through game modes: 2P → AI → JAM → EDIT. The current mode displays on the button.

7. Effects (XY Pads)

Two XY control surfaces at the bottom of the screen let you shape the sound in real time. Drag your finger across the pad to adjust two parameters simultaneously.

Left Pad

  • X-axis (horizontal): Reverb — adds space and depth
  • Y-axis (vertical): Delay — adds echoing repeats

Right Pad

  • X-axis (horizontal): Distortion — adds grit and saturation
  • Y-axis (vertical): Tremolo — modulates volume for a pulsing effect
Start subtle: A little goes a long way. Start from the bottom-left corner (no effects) and drag slowly to add flavor.

8. Edit Mode (Pattern Editor)

Edit mode lets you customize the 16-step pattern for any instrument.

Getting Started

  1. Tap MODE until it shows EDIT
  2. Tap any pad on the grid to select it for editing
  3. The step strip appears showing 16 steps (4 groups of 4)

Editing Steps

Tap any step to toggle it on or off. Active steps light up. The sequencer plays in solo mode — only the pad you're editing is audible, with a metronome click on downbeats (steps 1, 5, 9, 13) for timing reference.

Note Carousel (Melodic Instruments)

For melodic pads (Bass, Lead, Arp, Chord), tapping an active step opens the note carousel. Drag vertically to scroll through available notes. Notes are derived from the genre's scale, so everything stays musical.

Saving & Undoing

  • Auto-save: Changes apply instantly as you edit
  • OK: Confirms your edits and deselects the pad
  • Cancel: Reverts all changes you made since selecting the pad
  • Undo: Steps back through your edit history one change at a time
  • Switching pads: If you tap a different pad while editing, your current changes are automatically saved (you'll see a brief "Saved" indicator)

9. Tips & Tricks

  • Switch genres mid-game — The beat changes instantly while the game board stays. Try the same moves in different genres for completely different results.
  • Use Swing for groove — Even a small amount of Swing (15–25%) transforms a rigid beat into something that grooves.
  • Win break strategy — Your winning three cells play solo during the break. Try to win with a combination that sounds good together (e.g., Kick + Snare + Hi-Hat for a drum break).
  • Layer FX gradually — Start with the XY pads at the origin and slowly introduce reverb or delay as the game progresses and more layers build up.
  • TAP tempo — Tap along with a song or video to sync the beat's BPM in real time.
  • JAM mode for exploration — Use JAM mode to freely toggle pads on and off. Find combinations you like, then switch to 2P or AI to build up to them through gameplay.
  • Edit mode for custom beats — Customize every instrument's pattern in EDIT mode, then switch back to 2P to play a game with your custom beat.

10. Troubleshooting

No sound?

  • Check that your device is not on silent mode (the physical switch on the side of your iPhone)
  • Audio starts on the first tap — make sure you've tapped a pad
  • Try increasing the volume with the side buttons

Audio stopped after switching apps?

The app automatically recovers audio when you return from multitasking. If automatic recovery fails, a "Tap to resume audio" banner will appear — just tap it.

System requirements

Tic Tac Tone requires iOS 17.0 or later.
